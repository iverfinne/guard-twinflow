Digital Twins are data enabled digital replicas of physical assets or processes. A simple version of a Digital Twin can be used to gain insight across disciplines, roles and departments. By using real-time data, coupled with sophisticated models simulating optimal operations, the Digital Twin can be used as a tool for planning, operation and maintenance.

The benefits of digital twins

Digital twins offer many benefits, including the ability to simulate and analyze complex systems in real-time, identify potential problems, and optimize performance. They can also be used to improve communication and collaboration among stakeholders, and to create more efficient and sustainable processes.

For example, a digital twin of a manufacturing plant can be used to optimize production processes and reduce waste, while a digital twin of a hospital can be used to improve patient care and reduce the spread of infections. The possibilities are endless, and the benefits of digital twins are only just beginning to be realized.

Today's approach to digital twin solutions continues to fall short:

Costing millions with limited proofs of scale While digital twin technology has the potential to provide significant benefits to businesses, it is still a relatively new and emerging field. As a result, many digital twin solutions are currently expensive and may not have been proven at scale. However, it is important to note that the field of digital twin technology is rapidly evolving, and it is likely that costs will decrease and proofs of scale will become more common as the technology becomes more widely adopted and refined.
